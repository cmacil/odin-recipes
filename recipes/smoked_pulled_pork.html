Bubba’s Smoked Pulled Pork

Description

This is going to take a while -- four to five hours to do it right. It’s a waiting game while keeping temperatures relatively low, with indirect grilling and adding a little smokey flavor. You are going to need a charcoal-fired grill (we usually use a hamburger-shaped grill) or a smoker, plenty of charcoal, and wood chips soaking in water for smoke (beer for the chef). We also always use a dry rub, but it is not required.

Ingredients

  • 1 pork shoulder (Boston butt, 3.5 to 5 pounds)
  • 1/2 cup brown sugar
  • 1/2 tsp mustard powder
  • 1/2 tsp black pepper
  • 1 Tbsp paprika
  • 1 tsp salt

Steps

  1. Mix the above ingredients and use them to coat the cut of pork.
  2. Place the pork in the fridge for a few hours or overnight.
  3. Use a disposable aluminum drip pan in the center of the grill and place a handful or two of charcoal briquettes on each side of the pan.
  4. To get the grille started, use tongs to drop three or four red-hot charcoal briquettes on the briquettes on the four sides of the pan.
  5. Place the meat over the drip pan and use the bottom and top vents to regulate the temperature.
  6. You want an internal temp of 325-350 degrees.
  7. Every hour or so, you may need to add a few briquettes to each side of the pan.
  8. You can also add a few soaked wood chips, like apple or cherry wood.
  9. Once the center of the meat has reached an internal temperature of 195 degrees and the meat feels like it will fall apart, it is ready.
  10. Depending on how hot your grill has been and how large of a pork shoulder you are cooking, this can take 3-6 hours.
  11. Pull the meat off the heat carefully and place it in a large non-reactive cake pan.
  12. Cover with aluminum foil and wait an hour for it to cool off.
  13. When the meat is cool enough to touch, using clean hands, and a fork or two, pull the meat apart, drenching with the mop sauce as you go.
